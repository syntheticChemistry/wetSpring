## Core Idea

Anderson localization applies to ALL waves. Extend the framework from chemical
QS to mechanical/acoustic signaling in bacterial communities (Biophys Rev Lett
2025). Test whether B. subtilis K+ oscillations and other physical signals
follow the same dimensional rules.

## 6 Signaling Modalities

| Mode | Anderson Applies? | Dim. Dependence |
|------|:-----------------:|:---------------:|
| Chemical QS (AHL) | YES | Strong |
| Mechanical/acoustic wave | YES | Strong |
| Electromagnetic (biophoton) | YES | Moderate |
| Electrical (nanowire) | NO | Topology-dependent |
| Contact-dependent | NO | None |
| Membrane potential wave | YES | Strong |

## Key Findings

- 4/6 bacterial communication modes subject to Anderson localization
- Contact-dependent (Myxococcus C-signal) bypasses Anderson entirely
- Electrical nanowires follow network percolation, not Anderson
- Signaling portfolio scales with geometry: 3D biofilm = full portfolio,
  2D mat = contact only, planktonic = essentially none

## Headline

"This is WHY plankton don't signal" — not just chemical QS fails, but
ALL wave-based communication modes are suppressed in dilute 3D or 2D.
Only contact-dependent signaling (which requires cell-cell adjacency)
works reliably in non-3D geometries.
