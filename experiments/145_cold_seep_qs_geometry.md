## Key Finding

Frequency-division multiplexing: 34 QS types in one habitat = 34 independent
signaling channels. High-diversity 3D communities need MORE QS types than
low-diversity communities, analogous to radio frequencies in shared airspace.
The Anderson framework explains both WHY QS works (3D) and WHY multiplexing
is needed (high diversity = high disorder, different species use different
channels to avoid crosstalk).
