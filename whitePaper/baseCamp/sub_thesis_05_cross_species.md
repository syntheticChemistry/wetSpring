## Core Claim

Solo luxR receptors (eavesdroppers) are enriched in 3D mixed-species
habitats where the Anderson model predicts QS signals are delocalized
(extended). Cross-species QS networks are enabled by geometry and
constrained by localization physics.

## Cross-Species Communication Networks

- **Biofilm**: Dense communities → extended QS → multi-species coordination
- **Gut**: Enterobacteriaceae eavesdroppers detect neighbors via sdiA
- **Rhizosphere**: Rhizobial luxR detects AHL AND plant flavonoids
- **Cold seep**: 34 QS types → frequency-division multiplexing across species
- **Correlated disorder (Exp151)**: Spatial clustering of species creates
  correlated potential → W_c shifts upward → cross-species QS even easier
