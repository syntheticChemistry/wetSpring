## Core Claim

The Anderson localization framework predicts that rhizosphere biofilms
(3D dense, moderate diversity) are strongly QS-active. This has direct
implications for biological agriculture: QS-mediated biocontrol, plant
growth promotion, and nitrogen fixation are physics-favored in root-zone
environments.

## Agricultural Predictions from Anderson Framework

| Condition | Anderson Prediction | Practical Implication |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Root nodule (3D dense) | Strongly extended | QS biocontrol effective |
| Bulk soil (3D moderate) | Extended | QS functional but distance-limited |
| Leaf surface (2D) | Localized at high diversity | QS ineffective for diverse epiphytes |
| Hydroponic (3D dilute) | Depends on cell density | QS only above critical occupancy |
